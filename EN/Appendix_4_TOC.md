### Appendix Positioning

> This appendix focuses on the **Skyhook**: a space transportation system that uses rotating tethers for momentum exchange. A skyhook can serve as a **pre-validation platform** for ring-elevator technologies, a **supplementary deep-space launch method**, and a **transitional transport scheme** before ring-elevator completion. The appendix aims to systematically explain skyhook physical principles, engineering schemes, synergy with the ring elevator, and strategic value within the lunar industrial system.
>
> The key difference between this appendix and the main volume plus Appendices I, II, and III is that the skyhook does not contact the ground, has a much smaller structural scale than the ring elevator, and has lower material requirements. It can be independently deployed before ring-elevator completion, making it an important technical predecessor and capability supplement.

### Summary of Appendix IV Closing Remarks

This appendix systematically demonstrates the technical feasibility and strategic value of skyhooks as a space transportation system. Skyhooks and space ring elevators are not competitors; they form a **complementary, layered transport architecture**:

1. **Near term (years 5-10)**: Deploy rotating LEO skyhooks for low-cost cargo transport, validate key technologies such as on-orbit tether operations and momentum exchange, and accumulate engineering experience for ring elevators.

2. **Mid term (years 10-20)**: Deploy GEO skyhooks or two-stage skyhooks to enable rapid injection into Earth-Moon transfer trajectories and connect with lunar industry.

3. **Long term (after year 20)**: After ring-elevator completion, skyhooks can supplement ring-mounted ejectors or be deployed at higher locations (such as Earth-Moon L1) as deep-space skyhooks supporting missions beyond Mars.

Skyhook engineering feasibility is much higher than that of ring elevators, making skyhooks a key component in the ring-elevator strategy of progressive implementation from easier to harder stages. The planning in this appendix can serve as both technical reserve and pre-project for the ring-elevator program.
