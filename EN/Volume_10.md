# Volume 10: Economic Models and Commercialization Pathways

**Version**: 1.2 (Final Review)
**Compilation Date**: May 2026
**Currency Unit**: Renminbi (CNY), symbol: ¥


## 10.1 Preface

This volume is positioned as the **economic feasibility study and commercialization pathway planning** for the Space Ringed-Elevation Project, focusing on answering the following core questions:

> How much capital investment is required for the Space Ringed-Elevation Project? How should funding sources and financing structures be designed? After completion, can it generate sufficient cash flow to cover costs and sustain profitability? What is the investment payback period?

This volume follows:
- **Overview**: Investment estimation framework (about ¥15 trillion, invested in four phases)
- **Volume 8**: Detailed global construction timeline (about 28 years to full cable operation), total cost estimate for the transportation system (about ¥25–29 trillion), peak annual launch demand (about 1,000–1,500 times/year)
- **Volumes 1–7**: Detailed parameters of various technologies and engineering aspects

Core tasks of this volume:

1. Integrate the investment estimates from the Overview and Volume 8 to establish a refined, phased discounted cash flow (DCF) model.
2. Forecast market size and conduct pricing analysis for the core revenue sources of the Space Ringed-Elevation.
3. Design a diversified financing structure, covering sovereign funds, multilateral development banks, private capital, and operational pre-sales.
4. Evaluate investment return rates and cost recovery periods through multi-scenario analysis (optimistic/base/pessimistic).
5. Propose a progressive commercial operation pathway, enabling the project to start generating cash flow during the construction period.

### 10.2.7 Discounted Cash Flow Model

Based on the above phased investments, with the project start year as the base year (t=0), and using a discount rate r = 4%–6% (referencing the long-term social discount rate for global infrastructure projects), calculate Net Present Value (NPV) and Internal Rate of Return (IRR).

**CAPEX Time Distribution** (using median values for each phase):

| Year Range | CAPEX (¥ trillion) | Average Annual Investment (¥ trillion) |
|------------|:------------------:|:--------------------------------------:|
| Years 1–10 | 5.0 | 0.5 |
| Years 5–16 (overlaps with Phase 1) | 22.5 | 1.9 |
| Years 13–24 (overlaps with Phase 2) | 23.5 (median) | 2.0 |
| Years 25–28 | 5.5 | 1.4 |

**DCF Calculation Formula**:

$$
\text{NPV} = \sum_{t=0}^{T} \frac{C_t}{(1 + r)^t}
$$

where $C_t$ is the net cash flow (income − expenditure) in year t, and T is the evaluation period (this volume uses 50 years, covering the entire construction and early operation period).


## 10.3 Revenue Sources and Market Size Forecast

### 10.3.1 Core Revenue Sources

After completion, the main revenue sources for the Space Ringed-Elevation include:

1. **Earth-Moon and Deep Space Transportation Services** (largest revenue source)
2. **Global Communication and Data Relay Services**
3. **Space Solar Power Sales**
4. **Space Tourism and Habitation**
5. **On-orbit Manufacturing and Industrial Services**
6. **Scientific Research Platform Leasing**

### 10.3.2 Transportation Service Revenue—Largest Single Revenue Source

**Pricing Benchmark**: The current GEO satellite launch cost is about ¥50,000–100,000/kg (expendable rockets). The goal of the space elevator is to reduce the cost to 1%–5% of rockets, i.e., about ¥500–5,000/kg.

Conservative value: **¥2,000/kg** (about $278/kg, at 1 USD = 7.2 CNY).

**Capacity and Annual Revenue**:
- Single elevator annual effective capacity (uplink): ≈5,700 t/year (Volume 8, Section 8.2.1, effective payload 81.3 t × 70 round trips)
- 6 elevators total: ≈34,200 t/year (uplink)
- Downlink capacity (return cargo, recycled materials): 60% of uplink, about 20,500 t/year
- Annual total transportation revenue (conservative pricing ¥2,000/kg):
  \[
  34,200 \times 10^3 \times 2,000 + 20,500 \times 10^3 \times 1,000 = 68.4 \times 10^9 + 20.5 \times 10^9 \approx 890 \times 10^8 \, \text{¥/year} \approx 0.089 \, \text{¥ trillion/year}
  \]

Transportation revenue alone is about ¥0.089 trillion per year. To cover the total investment of ¥55–58 trillion (undiscounted), and without considering other revenue sources, it would take about 620–650 years. **This means that relying solely on transportation services cannot recover the investment within a reasonable period; other high value-added revenue sources are essential.**

**Time Cost Comparison**:

The above pricing analysis is based only on the unit mass transportation price (¥/kg), not including **time cost**. For certain types of cargo, the economic value implied by differences in transportation time may far exceed the difference in freight charges. The following compares the transportation time for typical missions between the space elevator and current mainstream rocket launches.

| Comparison Dimension | Heavy Rocket (Expendable) | Space Elevator | Description |
|---------------------|:------------------------:|:--------------:|-------------|
| Mission Preparation Cycle | 6–18 months (rocket assembly, testing, launch window) | ≤5 days/round trip (including docking) | Elevator is scheduled, rocket is custom mission |
| Actual Transportation Time | Several hours to days (orbital transfer) | ≈50 h (one way) | Actual travel time is similar |
| Total Delivery Time Including Waiting | **6–18 months** | **≤5 days** | Difference of two orders of magnitude |
| Typical Cargo Value (e.g., GEO Communication Satellite) | ¥1–5 billion/unit (including manufacturing and launch) | — | 500 kg satellite value about ¥200,000–1,000,000/kg |
| Time Backlog Cost (cargo value × waiting period/annual interest rate 5%) | ¥25–375 million/unit | ≈¥0.7 million/unit | Rocket's long waiting period leads to extremely high capital occupation cost |
| Time Premium per Unit Mass | ¥5,000–75,000/kg | Negligible | For high-value, time-sensitive cargo, time cost far exceeds freight itself |

**Physical Logic of Time Value**:

For high-value, time-sensitive cargo—
- **Emergency Relief Supplies** (disaster response, space station resupply): Each week of delay may cause hundreds of millions in economic loss or irreparable personnel risk
- **Scientific Samples** (biological experiments, material samples): Microgravity environments have strict time windows; missing the window means the entire batch of experiments is wasted
- **High-value Satellites** (communication, remote sensing, navigation): Waiting 18 months for launch means losing all commercial operating income during that period; with annual income per satellite at ¥100–500 million, time cost is about ¥150–750 million/unit
- **Personnel Transport**: The time value of astronauts and researchers cannot be measured by money alone; shortening the round-trip cycle greatly improves mission efficiency and safety, which rockets cannot match

The scheduled flight model of the space elevator gives it an irreplaceable advantage in transportation timeliness. Although this advantage is not explicitly included in the pricing strategy—the benchmark pricing in this volume (¥2,000/kg) is a conservative freight benchmark, not including time premium—in actual commercial operation, high-value, time-sensitive cargo can bear higher freight charges, providing important revenue elasticity for the elevator.

The construction period of the Space Ringed-Elevation is as long as 28 years, but some facilities can be gradually put into trial operation during construction, generating early cash flow and alleviating investment pressure.

- **Market Cultivation**: Allow potential customers to gradually adapt to and rely on the transportation and communication services of the Space Ringed-Elevation
- **Financing Credit Enhancement**: Stable cash flow from operating facilities can serve as collateral and credit endorsement for subsequent financing


## 10.4 Financing Structure Plan

### 10.4.1 Physical Logic of Financing

The investment scale of the Space Ringed-Elevation (about ¥55–58 trillion, invested over 28 years) far exceeds the fiscal capacity of any single country or enterprise, and a multilateral, diversified financing structure must be established. The average annual investment is about ¥2 trillion (peak period), accounting for about 0.03% of current global GDP (about $100 trillion), or 0.16% of China's GDP (about $18 trillion).

### 10.4.2 Financing Source Allocation (Base Scenario)

| Financing Source | Share | Amount (¥ trillion) | Description |
|------------------|:-----:|:------------------:|-------------|
| Sovereign Wealth Funds (China, Middle East, Norway, etc.) | 25% | ≈14–15 | Long-term equity investment |
| Multilateral Development Banks (AIIB, BRICS Bank, etc.) | 20% | ≈11–12 | Low-interest long-term loans |
| Aerospace Enterprise Consortium (SpaceX, China Aerospace, etc.) | 20% | ≈11–12 | Technology equity + cash |
| Green Climate Fund/Carbon Credit Financing | 10% | ≈5–6 | Space solar power replaces fossil energy |
| Private Capital (Pension Funds, Sovereign Funds, PE) | 15% | ≈8–9 | Pursuit of long-term stable returns |
| Operational Pre-sales and Bonds | 10% | ≈5–6 | Advance sale of transportation/energy contracts |

### 10.4.3 Return Mechanisms

- **Sovereign Funds and Multilateral Banks**: Enjoy priority debt repayment rights, annual interest rate referenced to SDR+1%–2%, recovery period 20–30 years.
- **Corporate Equity**: Entitled to operational profit dividends in proportion to investment, exit mechanism is public listing or agreed transfer after 30 years.
- **Green Fund and Carbon Credit**: Returns based on carbon emission reductions from space solar power replacing ground fossil energy.
- **Bond Investors**: Project bonds issued with specific revenue sources (such as transportation services, solar power) as collateral for future cash flow.

### 10.5.3 Key Economic Indicators in the Base Scenario

In the base scenario:
- **Average Annual Total Revenue**: about ¥1.5 trillion (after full cable operation)
- **Average Annual Operating Expenditure** (OPEX, including maintenance, personnel, fuel): about ¥0.3–0.5 trillion
- **Average Annual Net Operating Profit**: about ¥1.0–1.2 trillion
- **Undiscounted Payback Period**: about 35 years (from full cable operation, i.e., year 63)
- **Discounted Payback Period**: about 45 years (i.e., year 73)

**Conclusion**: In the base scenario, the Space Ringed-Elevation is a "potentially profitable" ultra-long-term infrastructure project, but not a short-term windfall project. Its value lies more in strategic dividends (reducing orbital access costs for all humanity) and external benefits (clean energy, research, safety). If space solar power and communication revenues exceed expectations, the investment payback period can be significantly shortened.


## 10.6 Economic Risk and Sensitivity Analysis

### 10.6.1 Key Sensitivity Factors

| Factor | Variation Range | Impact on NPV | Description |
|--------|:--------------:|:-------------:|-------------|
| Transportation Pricing | ±50% | Very Large | Transportation is the largest revenue source |
| Space Solar Power Grid Price | ±30% | Large | Second largest revenue source |
| Heavy Rocket Launch Cost | ±20% | Large | Accounts for 30%–40% of CAPEX |
| Lunar Manufacturing Capacity | 0–50% share | Medium | Can reduce CAPEX by 10%–15% |
| Rack Wear Life | ±50% | Medium | Affects OPEX and maintenance cycle |
| Construction Delay | +5 years | Large | Delays revenue, increases interest |

### 10.6.2 Risk Mitigation Strategies

- **Transportation Pricing Risk**: Lock in baseline revenue through long-term transportation contracts (10–20 year agreements with governments, satellite operators)
- **Power Price Risk**: Sign Contracts for Difference (CfD) with grid companies to guarantee minimum grid price
- **Launch Cost Risk**: Promote rocket reuse technology (target annual reuse ≥30 times), introduce new launch methods such as electromagnetic catapult
- **Construction Delay Risk**: Advance multiple critical paths in parallel (elevator construction, asteroid capture, lunar manufacturing) to avoid single point failures

## 10.8 Volume 10 Conclusion

This volume completes the economic feasibility study and commercialization pathway planning for the Space Ringed-Elevation Project. Core conclusions:

1. **Investment scale is significantly higher than previous estimates**: Based on the detailed construction parameters in Volume 8 and the first integration of all capital expenditures in this volume, the total project CAPEX is about ¥55–58 trillion (over 28 years), with an average annual investment of about ¥2 trillion. This scale is about 0.16% of China's GDP and is among the highest in global infrastructure investment history.
2. **Project may be profitable in the base scenario**: Benefiting from the combined effect of transportation, space solar power, communication, and tourism revenues, the base scenario annual revenue is about ¥1.5 trillion (after full cable operation), with a discounted payback period of about 45 years.
3. **Early operational revenue is key to cash flow management**: The first elevator can start generating revenue in year 10, with a cumulative recovery of about ¥2–3 trillion during construction, effectively alleviating later financing pressure.
4. **Multilateral financing structure is the only feasible solution**: The combination of sovereign funds, multilateral banks, corporate consortia, green climate funds, and private capital can match the financing needs of different stages during the 28-year construction period.
5. **Economic feasibility is highly dependent on revenue diversification**: Relying solely on transportation services cannot recover the investment (would take over 600 years); space solar power and communication services are decisive for the payback period.

The output parameters of this volume are sufficient to support the discussion in Volume 11 (Governance and International Coordination) on investment return distribution and the United Nations operation model.
