### Positioning of This Appendix

> This appendix is a parallel companion to the main volume of *The Space Ring Elevator Project*, but its positioning is not as a subsidiary of the main volume; rather, it is a high-altitude resident infrastructure system with diversified customers and independent profitability. Stratospheric platforms provide long-term residency at 20–50 km altitude via stratospheric airships or tethered aerostats, carry maintenance gondolas capable of climbing along the cable at any altitude from 0–100 km, and carry diverse payloads serving a range of needs including ring elevator maintenance, scientific observation, communications relay, disaster prevention warning, and high-altitude experimentation.
>
> Within the ring elevator system, stratospheric platforms are the core executors of maintenance, repair, rescue, and lightning protection for the atmospheric segment (0–100 km). But in the broader commercial picture, the ring elevator is only the largest and nearest of stratospheric platforms' customers—not the prerequisite for their existence. The independent commercial value of stratospheric platforms in meteorological monitoring, communications relay, and new materials testing makes them a high-value project worth investing in on its own merits, while also serving as a critical redundancy for risk control in the ring elevator project.
