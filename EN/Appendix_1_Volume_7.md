## 7.3.2 Water-oxygen-carbon-cycle closed path

Water, oxygen and carbon cycling are at the heart of life support systems. The design of the system follows the principle of "consumption recovery and reuse" and minimizes external supply dependence.

** (a) Water cycle**

- Refrigerated condensed water (atmosphere dehumidification in the chamber): → filter (5 μm+ activated carbon) + UV disinfected → directly returned to drinking water.
- Sanitary wastewater (show, laundry): → membrane bioreactive reactor (MBR, aperture 0.04 μm) + reverse osmosis (RO, desalinization rate 99%) → re-used for sanitary water and plant irrigation.
- Urea: distillation (low-temperature vacuum distillation, 40-50°C, reduction of energy consumption) + catalytic oxidation (TiO2/UV) → return to drinking water (98% recovery rate).
- Plant evaporation: → Refrigerated condensation (wetting of agricultural areas, foliage exchangers) → returned to drinking water.

** (b) Oxygen cycle**

- O2 Supply:
- **Physico-Chemical Paths (Main Program)**: Hydrolysis of Oxygen (solid polymer electrolytic tanks, 70% efficiency) O2 is injected into the atmosphere of residential areas after purification (catalysing hydrogen + drying).
-** Biological path (supplemented)**: Oxygen for plant light co-operation.
- CO2 Clear:
- ** Physico-Chemical Paths (Main Program)**: Molecular Sifting (13X Boiling Stone, CO2/N2 Select 10) + Sabatier Reaction ($CO_2 + 4H_2 \\rightarrow CH_4 + 2H_2O$, Ni-based Catalyst, 300-400°C, pressure 1-3 MPa). The generated H2O returns to the water cycle and CH4 is used as a carbon source or storage reserve for the CCVD.
-** Biological path (supplemented)**: Plant photoco2 absorption.

** (c) Carbon cycle**

- CO2 from crew breathing and industrial emissions (e.g., by-product CO2 from metal smelters, volume V).
- CO2 converted from Sabatier to CH4 and H2O, H2O for return, CH4 enters the carbon source reservoir - as a carbon source of FCCVD to synthesize CNT, or as an engine fuel for use in the Moon Lander.
- Plant biomass (non-edible part) produced CH4 and CO2 by anaerobic fermentation or pyrolysis, returning to the carbon cycle.

** (d) Microbiological control and biosafety**

In closed CELSS environments, micro-organisms are not accidental contamination, but are indispensable “hidden crews” in the material cycle. Useful micro-organisms (e.g. nitrification bacteria, fermenters) require active maintenance, while pathogens and fungi must be precisely controlled. Microbiological control follows a zoning strategy:

- ** Useful microbial area (active maintenance)**: the active slurry population in the membrane bioreactive reactor (MBR), the nitroglycerin bacteria in the symbiotic system of fish and the methane-producing population in anaerobic fermentation tanks are at the core of water treatment and solid waste conversion. The health status of these strains is monitored in real time through online sensors (pH, dissolved oxygen, oxidized level). In the forward post phase, the first bacterial species were pre-vaccinated from Earth in the form of frozen dry powder; after entering the industrial town phase, the system was self-sustaining and expanded on the basis of a local “bacterial stock”.
- ** Settlements (restricted control)**: mainly in terms of physical enzymes, supported by passive biological surveillance. All inner wall materials (SPUA seals, aluminium alloy panels, UHMWPE fabrics) shall be tested by ISO 846:2019 Plastics - Microbiological Effects Assessment [2], excluding microbial nutrient sources. High-exposure surfaces (door handles, control panels, arms) are coated with silver-containing antibacterial ion. The area ' s atmosphere is filtered by HEPA (99.97 per cent for 0.3 μm particulate efficiency) to control aerosol micro-organisms. The health balance of human symbiotic strains is maintained by no routine chemical disinfection.
- **Medical area (highest control) **: Isolated wards are under negative pressure maintenance (-15 Pa) and ventilated to the public ventilation system following double-stage treatment of Hepa+ UVC, 254 nm, irradiated dose 40 mJ/cm2.
- ** Dome farm (ecological control)**: using the “Friendly fungus sit-in” strategy - regularly immunosuppressed in trophic fluids with the advantage of biofilms at the plant's roots and on the foliage table, and thus competitively inhibiting the breeding of pathogens. No chemical microbicides are used to protect aquatic organisms of the symbiotic system of fish and vegetables.
- ** Passive monitoring network**: distributional micro-organisms sampled along ventilation pipes, water treatment modules, soil matrix and medical areas (1 ≥200 m2). 16S rRNA gene amplification rapid screening (<2 h results) per day at key nodes (medical area ventilation, drinking water outlet). Base-wide macrogenomic sequences are conducted once a week to establish a “baseline map of micro-organisms”; any microbial abundance deviation exceeds three standard deviations, the system automatically alerts and traces sources of contamination, and triggers all-base encryption sequences (one per day until the baseline is restored).

##7.5 Low-gravity Life Support and Gravity Design

In order to counter muscle atrophy and loss of bone density, long-standing astronauts and engineers are required to undergo daily targeted exercise. However, daily life in a low-gravity environment poses a dual physical and psychological challenge for the later influx of untrained travellers and migrants. They could not be asked to walk with “Kangaroo jumps” or to sleep with straps. Therefore, all daily living facilities must provide ordinary people with the experience as close as possible to Earth.

####7.5.1 Professional training facilities to combat physical degradation

This component is specifically designed for staff requiring a long-term presence (>3 months) to counter the loss of bone density and muscle contraction under 1/6 of gravity. Each crew member is required to undergo compulsory exercise of 1 h per day.

- ** Runner**: flexible belts with a waist (spring pull of about 5/6, simulated Earth gravity load). Moontop runners have matured on Earth — ISIS crews use them on a daily basis to counter microgravity bone loss, with a correspondingly reduced load of 1/6 gravity.
- ** Resistor training frame**: multiple sets of ballistic ropes (emulsive or spring steel), simulation of muscle groups trained in dud bells and bells. Fixed on the slides of floors and walls.
- ** Jump to training area**: with thick sponge mats (30 cm thick), crews can safely conduct vertical jump and roll training, exercise balance and front court system.

## # # 7.5.2 Optimization of ordinary human walking experience

** Rationale**: kangaroo ejection energy is efficient, but does not match the muscle memory and social habits of walking the Earth. The goal is to direct users to alternate steps.

** Engineering measures**:
- **High friction coefficient gravitational simulation floor**: soft surfaces with special texture (e.g. UHMWPE fabric floors with reverse hook structures) in residential areas and public spaces, friction factor > 0.8.
- ** Loaded reinforced shoes**: All persons are wearing specially designed indoor shoes in the area. The soles of the shoes are not simply weight-added (no change in mass), but are ** flex-fibre beams ** that are placed in the soles of the shoes. Fiber beams are loose and easy to save; when they are flat, they are compressed, and the bottom of the feet is required to overcome the impact and simulate the body feeling and muscle feedback of “stepping” under Earth gravity. The flexi-fibre beam is designed to fold 106 times, and the actual replacement period is determined on the basis of 80 per cent of the measured debacle life of the P3-H1 validation item in section 7.10.
- ** Effect**: Ingenious biomechanical design to guide ordinary people also to walk from their limbs in a step closer to Earth.

# # # # 7.5.3 Optimization of ordinary human sleep experience

** Rationale**: A “broadband” binding like a car seat belt can create a strong sense of restraint and anxiety for ordinary passengers. There is a need for a more moderate and natural fixation.

** Engineering measures**:
- **Soft slow-back grid sleep system**: replacement band. The bed frame is embedded in a slow-back memory cotton grid, which is like a large “dream net”.
- **Empirical**: When the user lies down, the grid sinks slowly and binds with the body ' s contours, providing a balanced, soft sense of package, rather than point binding. The grid space allows the arm to naturally stretch through the sleep, approaches the feeling of sleep under the feathers and effectively prevents the body from floating at will under low gravity.
- ** Supplement**: Rest areas are equipped with “high-density blankets” (high-density fibre fills) to provide a sense of pressure similar to the Earth's gravity and help to alleviate anxiety.

####7.5.4 Low-gravity like a toilet system — Splashing health unit

Under 1/6 gravitational force, fluid behaviour is dominated by surface tension, and any traditional toilet that relies on “fall” or “water seal” will be completely disabled by adhesive, hairy and uncontrolled spatter. The programme uses an integrated sanitation module that combines “closed collection” with “active cleaning”.

** (a) Splash-free collection system: high-speed gas vortex separators**

To abandon all traditional pit formations and to adopt a “rebutive” structure of a closed separation cavity.

1. **Conclusion and prepressure**: When the user is in position, the flexible ring of the toilet ring is automatically inflated and closed contact with the body is formed. The wind machine within the cavity is activated to form a negative pressure to ensure that the gas will not enter.
2. ** Zero exposure to high-speed separation**: two sets of critical air currents at the bottom of the vent.
- ** Ring-drive gas knife**: Producing a **-column high-speed rotating air flow** against the vent. It forms a mandatory "gas vortex."
- ** When excreta leaves the human body, whether solid, fluid or semi-fluorinated, immediately captured by high-speed vortex and accelerated centrifugal, shredded, dispersed and mixed in the air**.
- **Freight protection airscreen**: the inner arc wall along the separation cavity produces a **-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- All the mixtures dumped by the vortex hit the air screen and were bounced back to the centre of the air flow, ** to ensure that the walls of the cavity were not adhesive or spatter-free **.
3. ** Cyclone separation and packing**: The gas stream is holding the mixture down into a standard solid-liquid-gas tri-cyclophorus separators. The inner wall of the separators is coated with irradiated nano-painted (exposure angle > 150°) with a built-in razor and a mechanical clean-up of the wall at the end of each separation cycle. Solids and more dense liquids are thrown into the inner wall and slides along the wall into a one-time degradable collection bag at the bottom. Under pure liquid conditions, liquid droplets form liquid membranes in the inner wall following vortex flow centrifugal flow and are collected by a wall-covered chute; aerosol-grade fine droplets are captured by downstream defosterers (stereoline wires or electrostatic concentraters) and capture efficiency 99%. ** (b) Integrated anal cleaning system: air-water knife anal cleaning unit**

Anal cleaning is a central challenge to experience as a toilet. Under low-gravity, current washing also faces the problem of splashing and adsorption.

** Pre-open protective air flow**: When the cleaning unit reaches out, it first forms an outward flow of a “cipital form” and forms a one-way air flow shield in the anal area,** ensuring that any detachment or cleaning medium can only fly out and not splatter inside**.

# # # # 7.7.2 Monk Special Medical Risk

- ** Low gravitational adjustment period impairment**: New to the Moon crews are vulnerable to muscle pull and balance disorders in the short term under 1/6 gravitational force and are required to undergo adaptive training within 48 h after arrival, with adequate muscle laxatives and painkillers at medical stations.

- ** Emergency response plan for acute radiation exposure**: All non-essential persons entered storm shelters after SPE warning. In the event of accidental exposure of crews to SPE energy particles during EVA, acute radiation syndrome assessment and blood irritation factors are to be performed within 2 h.
- ** Vacuum exposure first aid**: In the case of short-term exposure of crew to the moon-surface vacuum (<90 seconds) as a result of the failure of the lunar uniform, treatment for pressure recompression, pure oxygen fluoride and high-pressure oxygen chamber is required immediately.
- **1/6 Medical operation adaptation under gravity**: 1/6 gravitational surgery is at risk of blood splatter and organ drift. The operating table must be equipped with a negative-pressure suction system and a magnetic surgical device, and the operator and assistant must be attached to a safety belt.

Note on Habitat

This volume contains a framework exploration of the physical space, life support systems and life support facilities necessary to sustain basic human survival in the extremes of the Moon from an engineering perspective. It covers, inter alia, the physical cycle, radiation protection, low-gravity physiology confrontation and the design of basic living facilities, and falls within the scope of “living works”.

The question of a truly complete human settlement — encompassing deep psychology in a long-term closed environment, the evolution of social structures, the cultural and spiritual needs of large groups of people, the human dimension of architectural design, the legal and ethical system, etc. — is an independent, professional and large-scale discipline that goes well beyond the scope of the annex. These issues must be studied in a systematic manner by the specialized forces in the fields of life sciences, social sciences, humanities and design, and this volume only serves as an entry point on the engineering side, providing for the physical base level constraints for follow-up.
