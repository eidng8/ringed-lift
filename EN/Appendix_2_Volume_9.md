### 9.1 Preface

This volume serves as the **economic feasibility demonstration and commercialization roadmap** for the stratospheric platform system, focused on answering the following core questions:

> How much capital investment does the stratospheric platform require? How should the funding sources and financing structure be designed? After completion, can it generate sufficient cash flow to cover costs and sustain profitability? How long is the investment payback period? What is the business model for the stratospheric platform independent of the Ring-Lift project? What incremental revenue is generated through synergy with the Ring-Lift?

This volume builds upon:
- **Volume 1 of this Appendix (Platform Overall Design)**: Platform technical parameters, mass, and energy requirements already established.
- **Volume 2 of this Appendix (Energy and Positioning Systems)**: Configuration and lifetime of photovoltaic, energy storage, and navigation equipment already established.
- **Volume 3 of this Appendix (Maintenance, Repair, and Rescue)**: Operation content and intervals for ring-elevator maintenance services already established.
- **Volume 4 of this Appendix (Research and Development Platform)**: Potential clients for scientific platform leasing already identified.
- **Volume 5 of this Appendix (Lightning Attraction and Protection)**: Lightning system maintenance costs already established.
- **Volume 6 of this Appendix (Communications Relay)**: Technical capability of communications services already established.
- **Volume 7 of this Appendix (Emergency, Protection, and Rescue)**: Emergency supply replacement intervals already established.
- **Volume 8 of this Appendix (Deployment and Maintenance)**: Deployment costs, maintenance costs, and equipment lifetime already established.
- **Main Volume X (Economic Model and Commercialization Roadmap)**: Provides the economic model framework for the Ring-Lift project; this volume follows its methodology.

Core tasks of this volume:

1. Integrate equipment lists, energy consumption, maintenance costs, and other data from the preceding eight volumes to build a phased input-output model for the stratospheric platform.
2. Design the core business model of the stratospheric platform, analyzing its revenue structure, cost composition, and profitability.
3. Propose diversified financing schemes covering sovereign funds, private capital, and service pre-sales.
4. Evaluate return on investment and cost recovery period through multi-scenario analysis (optimistic/baseline/pessimistic).
5. Analyze the standalone viability of the stratospheric platform independent of the Ring-Lift, and the incremental revenue from synergy.
6. Output key parameters for use by Volume 10 (Technical Interfaces) and explain the interface relationship with Main Volume X.

#### 9.3.2 Primary Revenue Source: Communications Relay and Data Backhaul

The stratospheric platform can serve as a high-altitude communications relay station, providing low-latency, high-bandwidth communications services to the following clients:

### 9.4 Investment Return Analysis

#### 9.4.1 Discounted Cash Flow Model

Taking the project launch year as the base year (t=0), social discount rate r = 5% (reference: infrastructure projects). Evaluation period: 15 years (platform design lifetime).

**CAPEX Time Distribution (single platform, baseline scenario)**:

| Year Range | CAPEX (10,000 CNY) | Description |
|:---|:---:|:---|
| Years 1–3 | 4,100 (mid-point value) | First platform + ground facilities |
| Years 3–5 | 365 (mid-point value) | Operations preparation |
| Year 5 | — | Stable revenue begins |

**Revenue and OPEX Time Distribution (single platform, baseline scenario)**:

| Year Range | Annual Revenue (10,000 CNY) | Annual OPEX (10,000 CNY) | Annual Net Cash Flow (10,000 CNY) |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| Years 1–3 | 0 | 0 | −1,367 (annual average CAPEX) |
| Years 3–5 | 375 (mid-point value) | 350 (mid-point value) | +25 |
| Years 5–10 | 750 | 550 | +200 |
| Years 10–15 | 900 (mature stage) | 600 | +300 |

**Net Present Value and Internal Rate of Return** (single platform):

- Total investment (CAPEX): ≈44.65 million CNY (mid-point value)
- 15-year cumulative net cash flow: ≈28.75 million CNY (undiscounted)
- NPV (r=5%): ≈−2 million CNY (slight loss)
- IRR: ≈4%–5%

**Conclusion**: A single platform operating independently (without ring-elevator maintenance contracts) has a negative NPV and poor economics, with payback period exceeding 15 years. Profitability requires ring-elevator maintenance contracts or formation-scale effects.

After adding ring-elevator maintenance contracts (annual +2 million CNY):

- Annual net cash flow (Years 5–15): +4 million → +5 million CNY
- 15-year cumulative net cash flow: ≈58.75 million CNY
- NPV (r=5%): ≈+9 million CNY
- IRR: ≈9%–11%

**Three-Platform Formation Baseline Scenario** (Note: due to overlapping coverage areas, communication and navigation service unit prices decrease for three platforms; total revenue is not a simple linear multiple):

- Total investment (CAPEX): ≈110 million CNY (mid-point value)
- Annual revenue (mature stage): 15 million CNY (three platforms combined)
- Annual OPEX: 12 million CNY (reduced after sharing)
- Annual net cash flow: +3 million CNY
- 15-year cumulative net cash flow: ≈35 million CNY
- NPV (r=5%): ≈−2 million CNY (near break-even)
- IRR: ≈5%–6%

After adding ring-elevator maintenance contracts (annual +4 million CNY, allocated across 6 elevators):

- Annual net cash flow: +7 million CNY
- 15-year cumulative net cash flow: ≈90 million CNY
- NPV (r=5%): ≈+18 million CNY
- IRR: ≈10%–12%

#### 9.5.2 Return Mechanisms

- **Government guidance fund / green finance**: Priority debt repayment rights; annual interest rate SDR+2%; payback period 8–10 years.
- **Venture capital**: Profit dividends proportional to contribution; exit mechanism via equity transfer or IPO after 5–7 years.
- **Commercial loans**: At market interest rates, collateralized by future service contracts.
- **Service pre-sales**: Future 3-year communications or maintenance services sold in advance at a discount (e.g., 80% of list price) as a cash flow supplement during the construction period.
- **Technology equity**: Equity calculated based on contribution valuation; participation in profit distribution.

### 9.6 Standalone Viability Independent of Ring-Lift

The stratospheric platform is designed with economic viability independent of the Ring-Lift project:

| Revenue Source | Dependent on Ring-Lift | Standalone Feasibility |
|:---|:---:|:---|
| Communications relay | No | High—can serve ocean, polar, and aviation sectors |
| Navigation augmentation | No | High—can serve as GNSS supplement |
| Meteorological data sales | No | High—unique high-altitude profile data |
| Scientific platform leasing | No | Medium—market development required |
| Emergency communications | No | High—government purchased services |
| Ring-elevator maintenance | **Yes** | **Low**—only available after ring-elevator completion |

**Conclusion**: In the scenario where the ring-elevator is not built or is cancelled, the stratospheric platform can sustain operations through diversified revenues from communications, navigation, meteorology, and scientific research, with single-platform annual revenue potentially reaching ¥5–9 million, essentially covering OPEX (¥5.3–7.9 million) and achieving marginal profit or break-even. The economies of scale are more pronounced for a three-platform formation. **The stratospheric platform does not depend on the Ring-Lift for survival, but the Ring-Lift significantly enhances its profitability.**


### 9.7 Risks and Mitigation

| Risk | Level | Mitigation Strategy |

| Ring-Lift project delay or cancellation | Medium | Strengthen non-Ring-Lift revenue sources (communications, meteorology, science); scale back to single-platform operations |

### 9.10 Volume 9 Conclusion

This volume completes the full construction of the stratospheric platform's economic model. Core conclusions:

1. **Single-platform standalone operations have poor economics**. Without Ring-Lift dependency, single-platform NPV is negative (approximately −2 million CNY), IRR approximately 4%–5%, with payback period exceeding 15 years. Profitability requires ring-elevator maintenance contracts or formation-scale effects.

2. **Ring-elevator maintenance is the largest incremental revenue source**. After adding ring-elevator maintenance contracts (annual ¥1.5–3 million), single-platform IRR improves to 9%–11% and NPV turns positive. Three-platform formation IRR can reach 10%–12%, with payback period approximately 8–12 years.

3. **The stratospheric platform has standalone viability**. Communications relay (¥2.5–5.3 million), navigation augmentation (¥0.5–2 million), meteorological data (¥0.5–1.5 million), and other diversified revenues can cover most OPEX, without Ring-Lift dependency for survival. The Ring-Lift is "the icing on the cake" rather than a prerequisite.

4. **Investment scale is moderate with diversified financing channels**. Single-platform total investment approximately ¥35–55 million; three-platform formation approximately ¥90–130 million; suitable for combined financing from government guidance funds, venture capital, and service pre-sales. Insurance premium rates have been adjusted downward in line with market reality.

5. **Economic sensitivity is highly dependent on ring-elevator maintenance and communications revenues**. ±30% variation in these two factors has a very large impact on IRR; they should be prioritized in operations.

6. **The interface with Main Volume X has been clarified**. The stratospheric platform's maintenance service fees constitute part of the ring-elevator's OPEX; shared anchor investment is allocated proportionally; communications relay can reduce ring-elevator's own communications costs.

This volume's output parameters are sufficient to support Volume 10 (Technical Interfaces) and economic model integration with Main Volume X. The stratospheric platform, as the Ring-Lift's "advance deployment" and "independent profit node," has had its economic feasibility fully demonstrated.
