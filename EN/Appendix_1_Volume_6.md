####6.7.2 Permanent programme: two-way circular cargo cable system

In very steep slopes where slopes exceed 30°, the train's railing is no longer sticky enough to provide pull — at this point, relying on gear rods. For a further increase in the slope (e.g., 30°-45° or steeper slopes on the inner wall of the ring), while the gear system can provide pull power through gear-cots, the quality and operational stability of the train formations is challenged. A two-way, circular cargo cable system** has been added to the permanent fixed-line transport of bulk cargo at pit bottom-water ice mining sites, working in a manner similar to that of a building-trucking elevator, which is not bound by a wheelchair at extreme slopes, the steeper it is, the better it is possible to balance gravity.

** (a) System rationale and applicability**

Cable systems have a two-car cycle equilibrium: two cargo cars are fixed at two ends of a high-strength cable, which bypasses a roller wheel at the pittop drive. When one car was travelling from the bottom of the pit, the other was travelling simultaneously from the top to the bottom of the pit — the uplifting energy consumption of the upper car was partially offset by the gravity of the lower car, and the driver was only supplementing the net upgrading power of the friction loss and the difference in the load of the two vehicles. This applies in particular to the 1/6-month gravity environment. The system is designed to be a one-way/two-way switching mode for the exclusive transport of cargo, with the carriage being open or sealed and suitable for mass material transport.
